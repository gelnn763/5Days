About 5Days

Why we built this.

The story behind 5Days and the problem it's trying to fix.

5Days is grounded in two specific laws: the Chicago Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance — one of the strongest tenant protection laws in the country — and the Illinois Eviction Act. Most tenants facing eviction have never heard of either. That's the gap we're closing.

When a landlord serves an eviction notice in Illinois, the clock starts immediately. A 5-day notice means five days. Not business days. Not days until you can get a lawyer. Five calendar days from when the notice was served — after which the landlord can file in court.

The landlord knows exactly what that notice means. They have property attorneys who draft these documents, who know which defenses to pre-empt, and who have been through eviction court many times. The tenant often has none of that. They have a piece of paper, a deadline they may not fully understand, and fear.

That asymmetry — of knowledge, access, and time — is the problem 5Days was built to address.

We built a tool that reads eviction notices the way a lawyer would, checks them against the Chicago RLTO and Illinois eviction statutes, flags procedural defects that could be valid legal defenses, and tells tenants exactly what to do next — in plain English, in under 60 seconds, for free.

We are not lawyers. 5Days is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. But we believe that access to legal information — understanding what your notice means, what your rights are, and who to call — should not be a privilege reserved for people who can afford an attorney.

Every system has hidden cheat codes — knowledge, access, networks, language, capital. Some people have them. Others don't. 5Days is a cheat code for the people who don't.
— The mission behind 5Days
